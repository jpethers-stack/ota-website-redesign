Compliance

Title IX

Old Town Academy does not discriminate on the basis of sex in its education programs and activities, and is committed to providing an environment free from sex-based discrimination for all students, families, and staff.

Statement of Non-Discrimination

In accordance with Title IX of the Education Amendments of 1972 and its implementing regulations, Old Town Academy does not discriminate on the basis of sex in admission to, access to, treatment in, or employment in its programs and activities. This prohibition against discrimination extends to sexual harassment and sexual violence.

Old Town Academy is committed to maintaining a learning and working environment that is free from discrimination based on sex, including sexual harassment. All students, employees, and other members of the school community are expected to conduct themselves in a manner that does not infringe upon the rights of others.

What Title IX Covers

Title IX protects students, employees, and other individuals from sex-based discrimination in all school programs and activities. This includes, but is not limited to, the following areas.

  • Admissions and enrollment
  • Academic programs and extracurricular activities
  • Athletics and physical education
  • Recruitment, hiring, and employment practices
  • Financial assistance and scholarships
  • Counseling and student services
  • Discipline and grievance procedures

Title IX also prohibits retaliation against any individual who files a complaint of sex discrimination, participates in an investigation, or opposes practices that they believe to be discriminatory.

Title IX Coordinator

Old Town Academy has designated a Title IX Coordinator to oversee compliance with Title IX and to respond to reports of sex-based discrimination. The Title IX Coordinator is responsible for coordinating the school's efforts to comply with Title IX, investigating complaints, and ensuring that appropriate steps are taken to stop discrimination, remedy its effects, and prevent its recurrence.

To report a concern or file a complaint, please contact the Title IX Coordinator.

How to File a Complaint

Any student, parent, employee, or other individual who believes they have been subjected to sex-based discrimination may file a complaint with the Title IX Coordinator. Complaints may be submitted in writing, by phone, by email, or in person.

The complaint process generally follows these steps.

  1. Report. Contact the Title IX Coordinator to report the concern. You may submit a written complaint or make an initial report verbally.
  2. Initial assessment. The Title IX Coordinator will review the report, discuss available options with the complainant, and determine the appropriate course of action.
  3. Investigation. If a formal complaint is filed, the school will conduct a prompt, thorough, and impartial investigation. Both parties will have the opportunity to present evidence and identify witnesses.
  4. Resolution. Following the investigation, the school will issue a written determination and take appropriate action to address the findings.
  5. Appeal. Both parties have the right to appeal the outcome of the investigation.

Old Town Academy will take interim measures as necessary to protect the safety of the parties involved and the integrity of the investigation. All complaints and investigations will be handled with the greatest degree of confidentiality possible.

Filing a Complaint with a Federal Agency

In addition to filing a complaint with the school, individuals may also file a complaint of sex discrimination with the U.S. Department of Education, Office for Civil Rights (OCR). Complaints to OCR must generally be filed within 180 calendar days of the last act of discrimination.
