Compliance

Uniform Complaint Procedures

Old Town Academy has the primary responsibility to ensure compliance with applicable state and federal laws and regulations. We have established Uniform Complaint Procedures (UCP) to address complaints alleging violations in these areas.

Overview

California's Uniform Complaint Procedures (UCP) provide a process for resolving complaints of unlawful discrimination, harassment, intimidation, or bullying, as well as complaints about violations of state or federal law governing certain educational programs. Old Town Academy is committed to resolving complaints promptly and fairly in accordance with California Education Code Section 234.1 and Title 5 of the California Code of Regulations, Sections 4600 through 4670.

What the UCP Covers

The UCP process may be used to file complaints regarding the following areas.

  • Discrimination, harassment, intimidation, or bullying based on actual or perceived characteristics including age, ancestry, color, ethnic group identification, gender expression, gender identity, gender, genetic information, mental or physical disability, nationality, national origin, race or ethnicity, religion, sex, sexual orientation, or on the basis of a person's association with a person or group with one or more of these actual or perceived characteristics
  • Violations of state or federal law in adult education, consolidated categorical aid programs, migrant education, career and technical education, child care and development programs, child nutrition programs, and special education programs
  • Failure to comply with the requirements of the Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP)
  • Failure to comply with physical education instructional minutes
  • Noncompliance with requirements related to the Every Student Succeeds Act
  • Complaints regarding Reasonable Accommodations to a lactating pupil

How to File a Complaint

Any individual, public agency, or organization may file a written complaint with Old Town Academy. Complaints must generally be filed no later than six months from the date the alleged violation occurred, or six months from the date the complainant first obtained knowledge of the facts of the alleged violation (whichever is later).

To file a complaint, please submit a written statement to the following.

The written complaint should include the following information.

  • The name and contact information of the complainant
  • The name of the school or individual against whom the complaint is filed
  • A description of the alleged violation, including specific facts and circumstances
  • The date or dates of the alleged violation
  • Any supporting documentation

Investigation and Timeline

Old Town Academy will investigate the complaint and provide a written decision to the complainant within 60 calendar days from the date the complaint was received. The investigation will include the following steps.

  1. Acknowledgment. The school will acknowledge receipt of the complaint and assign an investigator.
  2. Investigation. The investigator will gather and review relevant evidence, interview witnesses, and provide all parties an opportunity to present information. The complainant and the school's representative will have the opportunity to present evidence or information related to the complaint.
  3. Written decision. Within 60 calendar days, the school will issue a written decision that includes the findings of fact, the conclusion of law, the disposition of the complaint, the rationale for the disposition, corrective actions (if any), notice of the right to appeal, and the procedures to be followed for initiating an appeal.

Appeal Process

If the complainant is dissatisfied with the school's decision, they may appeal to the California Department of Education (CDE) within 15 calendar days of receiving the written decision. The appeal must include a copy of the original complaint and the school's written decision.

Appeals should be sent to the following.

California Department of Education
1430 N Street
Sacramento, CA 95814
Phone: (916) 319-0800
Website: cde.ca.gov/re/cp/uc

The CDE will review the appeal and issue a written decision within 60 calendar days of receipt, unless the parties agree to extend the timeline or the CDE documents exceptional circumstances that require an extension.

Civil Law Remedies

A complainant may pursue available civil law remedies outside of the UCP process. Complainants may seek assistance from mediation centers or public or private interest attorneys. Civil law remedies that may be imposed by a court include, but are not limited to, injunctions and restraining orders.

For complaints alleging discrimination, harassment, intimidation, or bullying, a complainant who is not satisfied with the resolution has the right to file a complaint with the Office for Civil Rights of the U.S. Department of Education.

Annual Notification

Old Town Academy provides annual written notification to students, employees, parents and guardians, school advisory committees, and other interested parties about the UCP process, including information about the complaint procedures and the opportunity to appeal. A copy of the UCP policy is available at the school office and on this website.
