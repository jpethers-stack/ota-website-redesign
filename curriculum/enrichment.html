Beyond the Classroom

Enrichment and After School

Learning at OTA does not stop when the bell rings. Our enrichment programs, PE curriculum, clubs, and field trips give students space to explore new interests, stay active, and strengthen the bonds of community.

Physical Education

Physical education is a core part of every student's week at Old Town Academy. Our PE program emphasizes fitness, sportsmanship, teamwork, and the development of lifelong healthy habits. Students participate in a variety of activities including team sports, individual fitness challenges, movement games, and cooperative activities. PE at OTA is designed to be inclusive and joyful, giving every child the opportunity to discover physical activities they enjoy.

Beyond physical fitness, PE reinforces the Core Virtues. Students practice respect on the field, perseverance through challenging workouts, and responsibility by caring for shared equipment and playing by the rules. Our PE teachers work closely with classroom teachers to ensure that the character lessons of the gym carry back into the classroom.

After-School Programs

OTA offers after-school programming that provides a safe, engaging environment for students whose families need extended care, as well as enrichment opportunities for students who want to dive deeper into specific interests. After-school programs are supervised by OTA staff and designed to complement the regular school day.

Program offerings vary by semester and may include homework help and academic tutoring, art and creative projects, outdoor games and sports, coding and technology clubs, and preparation for robotics competitions. The after-school program is a natural extension of OTA's commitment to the whole child, ensuring that the hours after dismissal are as purposeful and supportive as the hours before.

Clubs and Activities

Student clubs at OTA give learners the chance to pursue interests that go beyond the standard curriculum. Club offerings change each year based on student interest and teacher availability, but past clubs have included:

  • Art and illustration
  • Creative writing and journalism
  • Chess club
  • Environmental stewardship
  • Student leadership and government
  • Coding and game design

Clubs meet regularly and give students ownership over their learning in a low-stakes, high-engagement setting. For many students, clubs are where they find their people and discover passions that shape their path forward.

Field Trips and Community Connections

OTA takes full advantage of its San Diego location through regular field trips that extend classroom learning into the real world. Students have visited the San Diego Natural History Museum, Cabrillo National Monument, local farms and gardens, the Old Town State Historic Park, and regional science and technology centers. These trips are carefully planned to align with Core Knowledge content and PBL projects, ensuring that every outing deepens what students are learning in the classroom.

Community partnerships also bring the outside world into OTA. Guest speakers, visiting artists, local scientists, and parent volunteers enrich the school experience and show students the many paths that education can open. At OTA, the community is not separate from the school. It is an essential part of it.

Seasonal Events and Celebrations

Throughout the year, OTA hosts events that bring the whole community together: seasonal concerts, PBL showcases, family nights, the annual gala, and community service projects. These events celebrate student achievement, strengthen family connections, and reinforce the sense of belonging that makes OTA a special place to learn and grow.
