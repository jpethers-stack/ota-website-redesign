Innovation

Robotics Program

Osprey Robotics brings engineering, coding, and competitive problem-solving to life through the VEX IQ platform. Our students do not just learn about technology. They build it.

Osprey Robotics

Old Town Academy's Osprey Robotics program is one of the most exciting parts of the middle school experience. Built on the VEX IQ platform, the program gives students hands-on experience with mechanical design, sensor integration, programming, and competitive strategy. Students work in teams to design, build, and code robots that complete specific challenges, learning to iterate on their designs through testing and revision.

VEX IQ was chosen because it strikes the right balance between accessibility and depth. The snap-together structural system lets students focus on design concepts rather than getting bogged down in hardware assembly, while the programming environment (VEXcode) offers a progression from block-based coding to Python-based text coding. Students can start building on day one and still find new challenges months later.

Design Thinking in Action

Robotics at OTA follows the engineering design process: define the problem, brainstorm solutions, prototype, test, and refine. This cycle is not a poster on the wall. It is the daily rhythm of the program. Students sketch designs in engineering notebooks, calculate gear ratios, debate the tradeoffs between speed and torque, test their robots against the challenge specifications, and rebuild when things do not work.

The engineering notebook is a key component. Students document every decision, every test result, and every iteration. This practice builds scientific communication skills and teaches students to think systematically about why a design works (or does not work). It also reinforces the writing and documentation skills they develop across other subjects.

Coding and Automation

Every robot needs code to come alive, and programming is a core part of the Osprey Robotics experience. Students begin by coding autonomous routines that allow their robots to navigate courses, pick up objects, and complete tasks without human control. As they advance, they write more sophisticated programs that use sensor data (distance, color, touch, and gyroscope readings) to make real-time decisions.

Students also develop driver-control programs for competitive matches, learning to balance automated subroutines with manual operation. This dual focus (autonomous and driver-controlled) teaches students to think about both algorithmic precision and human-machine interaction.

Competition and Teamwork

Osprey Robotics teams compete in VEX IQ competitions throughout the San Diego region. These events are intense, collaborative, and deeply educational. Students learn to strategize under pressure, adapt to unexpected conditions, and work alongside (and against) teams from other schools. Competition days are some of the most memorable experiences of students' time at OTA.

But the value of the program extends far beyond trophies. Students learn project management, time allocation, communication under stress, and the humility that comes from watching a design fail in front of an audience. They also learn to celebrate each other's successes and support teammates through setbacks. The Core Virtues of perseverance and integrity are put to the test every time a robot takes the field.

Who Participates

The robotics program is focused on OTA's middle school students (grades 6 through 8), with introductory exposure to robotics concepts woven into the broader STEM curriculum in earlier grades. Middle schoolers who join Osprey Robotics commit to regular practice sessions, engineering notebook documentation, and competition preparation throughout the season. No prior coding or engineering experience is required. Curiosity and willingness to learn are the only prerequisites.
