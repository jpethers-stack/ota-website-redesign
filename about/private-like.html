Public School, Private Feel

A Private-Like Education

Everything families love about private schools (small classes, enriched curriculum, personal attention) with everything they need from public education (free, open enrollment, accountability).

The Best of Both Worlds

When families visit Old Town Academy for the first time, they often say the same thing: "This feels like a private school." The campus is small and well kept. Teachers greet students by name at the door. Classrooms are calm and purposeful. Parents are welcomed as partners, not managed as visitors.

But OTA is not a private school. We are a free, public charter school open to any family in San Diego. There is no tuition, no entrance exam, and no prerequisite. The quality families experience here is not reserved for those who can afford it.

Small Classes, Big Impact

Our 15:1 student-teacher ratio is the foundation of the OTA experience. In a class of fifteen, a teacher can see when a student is stuck before frustration sets in. They can challenge a student who is ready to go further. They can build the kind of trust that makes students willing to take risks, ask questions, and push through difficulty.

Small class sizes also mean more time for each family. Teachers at OTA know not just their students, but their siblings, their parents, and the circumstances that shape their learning. That knowledge turns a generic education into a personal one.

An Enriched Curriculum

Private schools are often known for going beyond the basics. At OTA, we do the same. Every student receives Core Knowledge instruction in reading, writing, math, science, and history. But our program extends well beyond core academics.

  • Instrumental Music: Every student learns an instrument, building discipline, teamwork, and creative expression.
  • STEM and Robotics: Hands-on projects and a competitive robotics program develop engineering thinking from an early age.
  • Core Virtues: Daily character education reinforces the habits and values that underpin a meaningful life.
  • Physical Education: Regular PE supports health, coordination, and the ability to focus during academic time.
  • Project-Based Learning: Students apply what they learn to real problems, building confidence in their ability to make a difference.

A Community That Invests in Itself

One of the hallmarks of great private schools is a strong sense of community. At OTA, that community is built not by exclusivity, but by shared purpose. Our families choose this school because they believe in what it stands for. They show up to volunteer, attend performances, support fundraisers, and collaborate with teachers.

The result is a school where everyone is pulling in the same direction. Students feel it. Teachers feel it. And visitors notice it the moment they walk through the gate.

Schedule a tour and see for yourself what a private-like public education looks like in practice.
